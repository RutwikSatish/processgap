The Problem
Every company knows which processes feel slow. Few know which step costs the most.
Process mining tools like Celonis and UiPath automatically discover process flows from ERP and CRM event logs — but they cost $80K–$500K to implement and require system integration work. Management consulting engagements for process analysis run $200K or more. Companies between $5M and $500M in revenue typically have neither.

The result: the same bottlenecks persist for years because nobody has quantified them well enough to justify fixing them.

What ProcessGap Does
A structured VSM and bottleneck analysis in 10 minutes — manual input, free, no integration required.
You enter each process step with its cycle time, wait time, resource count, and hourly cost. ProcessGap classifies each step as VA, NNVA, or NVA, identifies the binding constraint using utilization analysis, quantifies estimated annual waste cost, and models the ROI of fixing each bottleneck.

This is not automated process mining from system logs. It is a structured analysis framework applied to process data you supply. The output quality depends on the accuracy of your inputs.
Note: ProcessGap uses manual inputs, not event log extraction. Results are estimates based on the cycle times, wait times, and costs you enter. The recovery potential figure uses conservative rates from Six Sigma CoQ literature (70% of NVA cost, 60% of wait cost, 80% of rework cost) — not a guarantee of savings.

Bottleneck Detection
Utilization = daily demand / step capacity. The step where utilization exceeds 100% is your constraint. Steps at 80–100% are at risk under demand spikes.
Utilization Analysis
04
ROI Estimate
Conservative recovery estimate: 70% of NVA step cost, 60% of wait cost, 80% of rework cost. Based on Six Sigma Cost of Quality framework.
CoQ Framework
